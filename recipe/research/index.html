Hong Kong Style Egg Tarts

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all purpose flour (fluffed and spooned into measuring cup)
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 12 tablespoons unsalted butter (slightly softened)
  • 2 tablespoons cold water
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk (at room temperature)
  • 3 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Add cold water and bring the dough together. Wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  3. Roll dough to a rectangle, fold, turn, and repeat. Chill for 1 hour.
  4. Meanwhile, dissolve sugar in hot water, cool, then whisk in milk, eggs, and vanilla. Strain.
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F/190°C. Roll dough 5mm thick, cut circles, and line tart tins.
  6. Fill shells three quarters full with custard. Reduce oven to 350°F/180°C, bake 26–29 min.
  7. Cool at least 10 minutes before serving.

Source: The Woks of Life
